## 1. WHO Statistics Correction (CRITICAL)

**Old figure (used pre-March 29):** 466 million people with disabling hearing loss (WHO)  
**Correct current figure:** **430 million** (WHO 2021 World Report on Hearing / March 2026 Fact Sheet)

**Why different:**
- The 466M figure came from the pre-2021 WHO fact sheet using a **>40dB threshold** for "disabling hearing loss" in adults
- In 2021, WHO published the World Report on Hearing, which lowered the threshold to **>35dB** and updated epidemiological data → **430 million**
- The March 2026 WHO fact sheet confirms: "Over 5% of the world's population – or 430 million people – require rehabilitation to address their disabling hearing loss"

**Additional WHO figures (current):**
- 1.5 billion people globally have some degree of hearing loss
- 34 million children with disabling hearing loss
- By 2050: projected 700 million with disabling hearing loss (previously projected 900M under old methodology)
- By 2050: 2.5 billion with some degree of hearing loss
- Annual global cost of unaddressed hearing loss: **US $980 billion** (up from $750B in old reports)
- 80% of people with disabling hearing loss live in low- and middle-income countries
- **95.1 million** children aged 5-19 live with hearing loss (updated from earlier "34 million" which was children with disabling hearing loss only)

**Source:** https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/deafness-and-hearing-loss (updated March 3, 2026)

## 2. EECC / European Accessibility Act — Latest Status

### RTT Implementation Timeline (Dec 2024 EC Report)
- **Ireland:** First EU country with native RTT (2025) ✅
- **Malta, Netherlands:** App-based RTT capability
- **Germany:** Some PSAPs potentially from June 2025; all PSAPs by June 2027
- **Finland:** Delayed from Summer 2025 to 2027
- **Majority of Member States:** Target native RTT by June 2027 (but roadmaps are "indicative and subject to delays")

### UN CRPD Committee Review of the EU (March 2025)
- Expressed **"serious concern"** about delayed implementation of accessible 112
- Called on EU to **accelerate** development of standards for accessible emergency communications
- Specifically demanded: accessible emergency communications **in National Sign Languages through Total Conversation**
- Demanded **interoperability** across Member States
- New harmonized standards expected January 2027 — **only months before the legal deadline**
- EUD called this "a direct threat to the safety of deaf people"

### European Accessibility Act (EAA) — Applied June 28, 2025
- Now in force across EU
- Emergency services: people with communication difficulties must access 112 via voice, text, OR video in real time
- PSAPs must respond with same communication method as received (text→text, video→video)

### Open Letter (April 2025) — EENA + EDF
- Called out Poland, Cyprus, Malta for NOT implementing Advanced Mobile Location (AML)
- Called for accessible 112 means to include **equivalent caller location information**
- Noted many accessibility features (SMS, apps, relay services) **don't send caller location** — clear EECC breach

**Why this matters for Medak pitch:** The regulatory pressure is ENORMOUS and getting more urgent. The gap between EU legal requirements and implementation reality is widening. Medak's "zero PSAP upgrade" approach is even more valuable given most PSAPs can't meet the 2027 deadline.
