# 🎭 Pitch Delivery Guide — How to *Perform* the Pitch

> This document is about **how** to say it, not what to say.  
> A perfect script delivered badly loses. A good script delivered brilliantly wins.  
> Read once, internalize, then forget it. Deliver naturally.

---

## The Overall Arc: 3 Emotional Beats

```
PAIN          → HOPE         → CONVICTION
(Slides 1-2)    (Slides 3-5)   (Slides 6-12)

Make them feel  Show the fix.   Prove you can
the problem.    Excite them.    deliver it.
```

Judges should feel something in the first 90 seconds. If they don't, you've lost them.

---

## Slide-by-Slide Delivery Notes

### Slide 1 — Opening: ANA (30 seconds)

**The most important 30 seconds of the entire pitch.**

- Walk to the center. Stand still. Make eye contact.
- Start with silence. 2-3 full seconds. Don't rush.
- "Vaš stan gori." — Say it **slowly**. Pause. Let it land.
- "Zgrabi telefon." — Slightly faster. Urgency building.
- "Birate 112." — Normal pace.
- "Ali ne možete da govorite." — **Slow again**. Long pause after.

The silence after "ne možete da govorite" is the most powerful moment of the entire pitch. Hold it for 2 seconds minimum. Let them feel it.

**Body language:**
- Hands still at sides. No fidgeting.
- Eyes: scan the judges slowly — one, then the next.
- Voice: conversational, not theatrical. Real, not performed.

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ Don't rush through the opening
- ❌ Don't smile during this slide — it's not a happy moment
- ❌ Don't look at the slide — look at the judges

---

### Slide 2 — Numbers (80 seconds)

**Deliver numbers with weight, not speed.**

- "430 miliona" — pause after. Let them process.
- "84 hiljade" — shorter pause.
- "Nula zemalja." — **Full stop. Look at them.**

The "zero countries" landing is important. This slide should feel like an indictment, not a statistics lecture.

**Pacing:**
- Each stat deserves 3-4 seconds of silence after it
- The transition from numbers to Stefan's story is a gear shift — slow down

**Stefan story:** Tell it like you know Stefan. Like he's real. The detail "hvata se za levo rame" (grabs his left shoulder) — say it with your hand on your own shoulder.

---

### Slide 3 — Competition (50 seconds)

**Tone shift: confident, not emotional.**

You've established the problem. Now you're taking control. Your body language should change — stand straighter, speak more crisply.

- The competitive table goes fast — don't explain every row
- Land on "Medak: glasovni poziv na 112. Nula promena na strani hitne službe." — this is the differentiating point
- "Zvuči jednostavno. Nismo mi prvi koji su to pomislili." — slight smile. Self-awareness is charming.

---

### Slide 4 — Solution (60 seconds)

**This is where energy increases.**

The presenter's voice should be slightly faster and more energized here. You've found the answer. You're excited.

- "Korisnik pritisne SOS" — demonstrate with your own phone/hand
- "Za 38 sekundi" — hold up three fingers, then eight. Visual anchor.
- The architecture overview is one breath: "Kamera vidi. AI razume. Agenti zovu. Dispečer odgovara."

---

### Slide 5 — DEMO (90 seconds)

**Complete silence during the demo. This is the law.**

```
Lead Presenter: "Gledajte." [steps back]
                [points to phone]
                [SILENCE — 42 seconds of demo]
                [green screen appears]
                "Trideset osam sekundi."
```

- Do NOT narrate during the demo
- Do NOT say "as you can see" or "here it connects to"
- Stand still. Look at the judges. Let the system speak.
- When the green screen appears — small pause. Then: "Trideset osam sekundi." Quietly.

The 2-second pause before "Trideset osam sekundi" is everything. Practice this exact moment.

---

### Slide 6 — Architecture (60 seconds)

**Transition to Tech Lead. Handoff should feel natural.**

Lead Presenter: "A sada — Marko će objasniti kako ovo radi ispod haube."

Tech Lead takes over. Different energy — precise, measured, confident.

Key delivery notes for Tech Lead:
- Don't apologize for complexity. Own it.
- "Deterministička state mašina" — say it like it's obviously the right choice
- The confidence scoring formula: write it in the air if you need to ("trideset pet posto, dvadeset pet posto...")
- "Nikakav LLM u procesu donošenja odluka" — emphasize. This is responsible AI.

---

### Slide 7 — Implementation Problem (50 seconds)

**This is the story of a smart team who solved a hard problem.**

Lean forward slightly. This is the "we figured something out" moment.

- "Pronašli smo bag" — slight smile. Pride, not embarrassment.
- Walk through the before/after quickly — this isn't the main point
- The main point is: "Confidence scores su sada zavisni. Svakih 500 milisekundi se ažuriraju."
- Land on: "Naše rešenje je specifično za ovaj problem, ne opšte."

Then hand off back to Lead Presenter naturally.

---

### Slide 8 — User Research (50 seconds)

**Bring authority without arrogance.**

- "N=18 je mali uzorak. Ali kombinujemo ga sa..." — acknowledge it before they can object
- The EENA and AccesSOS data: cite the organizations, not just the numbers
- "83% bi koristilo AI relay" — hold eye contact. This validates everything.
- "Primarni strah: tačnost. Ne privatnost." — slight surprise in your voice. Like you expected them to care about privacy and they surprised you.

---

### Slides 9-11 — Business + Scaling + Research (fast section)

**Pick up pace. These slides support, they don't impress.**

Don't slow down here. Judges have already formed their opinion. These slides add credibility.

- Numbers: confident delivery, no hedging
- EECC: "Propis kaže da mora da postoji. Nema ko da to uradi. Mi to radimo."
- "Serbia kao EU pristupni kandidat mora da uskladi EECC" — drop this in Q9 if there's a business questions

---

### Slide 12 — Close (30 seconds)

**Go back to Ana. Full circle. Quiet power.**

The room should be completely silent.

- "Ana sada ima Medak na svom telefonu."
- Pause 2 seconds.
- "Gledamo decu. Gledamo starije. Gledamo Milicu. Sa Medakom — može."
- Pause 3 seconds.
- Look directly at the judges: "Hvala."

**Do not say anything after "Hvala."** Bow slightly. Return to position. Wait.

The silence after the close is as important as the close itself.

## Timing Discipline

**The 10-minute limit is absolute.** Judges can penalize for going over.

Use the PRESENTER_POCKET_CARD.md for timing reference.

**If you feel yourself falling behind:**
- Don't speed up — drop content (see ✂️ marks in pitch script)
- "Preskočiću detalje ovde jer imamo limitirano vreme" — acceptable to say
- Never apologize for skipping — just skip

**If you finish early:**
- Slow down the close
- Add Jelena persona (Slide 2 expansion — 15 seconds)
- Never add ad-libbed content you haven't rehearsed

## Q&A Delivery

**After judges ask:** Brief pause (2 seconds) before answering. Shows you're thinking, not just waiting to talk.

**For hostile questions:** "Dobro pitanje — i svesni smo ograničenja." Then pivot. Never be defensive.

**For questions you can't answer:** "To je izvan okvira ovog PoC-a, ali na production roadmapu je za Fazu 2." Then name the Phase 2 briefly.

**Tone throughout Q&A:** Calm, collaborative. You're solving a problem together, not defending a dissertation.

---

## The Three-Word Mantra (Repeat Before Going On Stage)

```
SLOW. STILL. CERTAIN.
```

- **Slow:** Speech pace 20% slower than you think is right
- **Still:** Hands down, feet planted, eyes steady
- **Certain:** You've done the work. You know the answers. This is a conversation, not an exam.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid on Stage

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Rushing the opening | Start with 3-second silence. No exceptions. |
| Narrating during demo | Say "Gledajte." Then be quiet. |
| Looking at the slide | Glance → back to judges within 2 seconds |
| Saying "um" and "uh" | Pause instead. Silence > filler |
| Apologizing for anything | Own every decision, acknowledge limitations confidently |
| Speeding up when nervous | Do the opposite. Slow down when nervous. |
| Fidgeting hands | Hands at sides or one hand with the clicker. Nothing else. |
| Explaining every technical detail | Hit key points, stop. Judges can ask. |
| Weak handoff between presenters | Rehearse the transition 5× until smooth |
