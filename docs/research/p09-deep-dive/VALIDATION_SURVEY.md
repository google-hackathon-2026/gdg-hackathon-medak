# Emergency Accessibility Validation Survey

## Purpose
Demonstrate to hackathon judges that there is real demand for an AI-powered emergency relay for deaf/speech-impaired people. This Google Form should be shared with:
1. Deaf/HoH community organizations (Savez gluvih i nagluvih Srbije)
2. Emergency services professionals
3. Disability rights advocates
4. General public (for awareness data)

---

### Section 3: The Solution

**[Show description]:**
```
Imagine an app on your phone that works like this:
1. You type or speak your emergency (in any language)
2. AI understands what you need (medical, fire, police)
3. AI calls 112 FOR you — speaking to the dispatcher in real-time
4. You see a live transcript of the conversation on your screen
5. If the dispatcher asks a question, it appears on your screen and you can type your answer
6. The AI speaks your answer to the dispatcher
7. You get a notification: "🚑 Ambulance dispatched — ETA 8 minutes"

No voice needed from you. The AI handles the call.
```

**Q8. How useful would this solution be for you or someone you know?** (Linear scale 1-5)
- 1 = Not useful at all
- 5 = Extremely useful

**Q9. Would you trust an AI to make an emergency call on your behalf?** (Multiple choice)

**Q10. What features would be most important to you?** (Checkboxes — select all that apply)

- Support for sign language input (camera)
- Multiple language support
- Works without internet (offline mode)
- Location sharing with dispatcher
- Emergency contacts notification
- Simple, large-button interface

## Expected Results (for pitch)

Based on existing research (EENA, Gallaudet), we can predict:
- **80%+** of deaf respondents will rate confidence at 1-2/5 for reaching emergency services
- **90%+** will rate the solution 4-5/5 useful
